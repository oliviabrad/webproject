About Eurovision

What is Eurovision?

The Eurovision Song Contest is when countries all around Europe conpeat in a music contest. The contest started in 1956 to promote a new international broadcasting company European Broadcasting Union (EBU), and to bring together European nations after World War II. The contest was origanlly 14 nations, sining mainly waltz music, in a small concert hall. Since then there have been 52 nations that have participated in the contest with the music spanning over various genres and the contests filling packed stadiums. Winners are chosen based on juries from each country and votes by the public audience, because of this some winners, like Ukraine in 2016, win the contest depsite not getting the popular vote with either the juries or the public. Eurovision gives budding artists international recognition, even if they don't win. Below are some parcipants who you may have heard of (winners are in bold).
